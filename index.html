Featured Content

Online feeds are not neutral. They're designed to capture attention, and that can amplify appearance pressure, comparison, and “perfect” standards that aren't realistic in everyday life.

The featured video follows a young girl who changes her appearance by editing photos to fit “perfect” social media standards. As the edits become more extreme, it highlights how filters and validation can harm self-image.

Three reminders to keep in mind while scrolling:

  • What you see is often edited, filtered, or carefully selected.
  • Engagement (likes/comments) is not a measure of personal worth.
  • Confidence grows when identity is built offline too.

Learn more: American Psychological Association: Social media topics

Secondary Content

Myth vs Reality

Myth: Everyone else has a perfect life

Reality: You're only seeing filtered “best moments.” Real life is messy and that’s normal.

Myth: Likes and comments measure your worth

Reality: Your value has nothing to do with engagement metrics. You matter regardless of likes.

Myth: You need to look a certain way to be beautiful

Reality: Beauty is diverse. Real beauty isn't filtered, edited, or one-size-fits-all.

Confidence beyond the screen

Small habits that help reduce comparison and build self-trust:

  • Curate your feed: mute accounts that trigger comparison.
  • Follow creators who share diverse, realistic content.
  • Do one offline activity that makes you feel capable (walk, journaling, hobby).
